I'm currently a third year medical student at Penn State College of Medicine.

I graduated from Cornell University with a B.S. in biometry & statistics . Between graduation and starting medical school, I worked as a data analyst at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in the Epidemiology & Biostatistics Department.

Research


Predicting survival for patients with cancer

Penn State COM, Hershey, PA; PI: Nicholas Zaorsky, MD MS

Multiple projects that use a "big-data" and machine learning approach to evaluate mortality in cancer patients

  • Identification and validation of the prognostic impact of metastatic prostate cancer phenotypes
  • Pan-cancer analysis of prognostic metastatic phenotypes
  • Survival after palliative radiation therapy for cancer: The METSSS model

Image analysis of mammograms

MSKCC, NYC, NY; PI: Malcolm Pike, PhD

Develop imaging pipeline in R that implements pre-processing, clustering, and machine learning algorithms to obtain predicted breast density readings from mammograms. Published in NPJ Breast Cancer .

The repercussions of concussions: Impact of self-reported diagnosed concussions on temporal discounting

Cornell University, Ithaca, NY; PI: Valerie Reyna, PhD

  • Presented at 15th Annual Meeting of the Society for NeuroEconomics in Toronto, Canada (Nov. 2017)
  • Manuscript under review in Journal of Athletic Training
